# EXPLORE ARTICLES

**Source:** https://support.myclickfunnels.com/docs/domain-terminology

---

When it comes to establishing your presence on the web, understanding domain terminology is crucial. Your domain, also known as your web address or URL, serves as the entry point for visitors to access your website. Below, we’ve compiled a list of common domain-related terms to help deepen your understanding.

A Record

An A record is a DNS setting that associates a domain name with a specific IPv4 address. It essentially points your domain to a particular destination on the internet.

AAAA Record

Unlike A records, AAAA records are DNS settings that link a domain name to an IPv6 address, enabling connectivity over IPv6 networks.

CAA Record

CAA records dictate which certificate authorities are authorized to issue SSL certificates for a domain, bolstering security and trust online.

CNAME Record

CNAME records serve as aliases, allowing you to point subdomains to other domain names seamlessly.

DKIM Record

DKIM records authenticate email messages, ensuring they are legitimately sent from the domain owner and not altered during transit, thus mitigating spam and phishing risks.

DMARC Record

DMARC records enhance email security by specifying actions for email providers to take when messages fail authentication checks, reducing the likelihood of fraudulent emails.

Domain Name Systems (DNS)

DNS is akin to the internet’s phonebook, translating human-readable domain names into machine-readable IP addresses, and facilitating website access.

Domain Registrar

Domain registrars are companies or organizations responsible for registering and managing domain names on behalf of website owners. They provide services including domain registration, renewal, and management, facilitating the process of establishing and maintaining a website’s online identity.

Email Hosting

Email hosting services enable the creation of custom email addresses associated with your domain, facilitating professional communication with clients and customers.

Internet Corporation for Assigned Names and Numbers (ICANN)

ICANN is a global organization responsible for overseeing and coordinating the administration of domain names and internet protocol addresses, ensuring the stable and secure operation of the internet infrastructure.

International Domain

An international domain is a web address tailored to specific countries or regions, allowing businesses to localize their online presence and cater to diverse audiences.

Internet Protocol Address (IP Address)

An IP address is a unique numerical label assigned to each device connected to a computer network, facilitating communication and data exchange over the internet.

MX Record

MX records specify the mail server responsible for receiving email messages on behalf of a domain, ensuring reliable email delivery.

Nameserver

Nameservers are specialized servers responsible for translating domain names into corresponding IP addresses, facilitating the routing of internet traffic to the correct web servers. They play a crucial role in directing visitors to the appropriate website associated with a domain name.

Root Domain

The root domain is the base domain name without any prefixes or subdomains, forming the foundation of your website’s address.

SPF Record

SPF records validate email senders and prevent email spoofing by specifying which servers are authorized to send emails on behalf of a domain.

Subdomain

A subdomain is a distinct section of a website identified by a prefix preceding the domain name, often used to categorize content or create separate web environments.

Time to Live (TTL)

TTL specifies the duration for which DNS records are cached by servers and devices, influencing how quickly changes to DNS settings propagate across the internet.

TXT Record

TXT records contain arbitrary text information associated with a domain, commonly used for verification, authentication, or configuration purposes.

In summary, a solid understanding of domain terminology is crucial for the effective management of online assets. By becoming familiar with concepts such as DNS records, domain registration, internet protocols, and email authentication techniques like DKIM and SPF, individuals can confidently navigate domain-related tasks, ensuring the smooth operation and security of their online presence.
